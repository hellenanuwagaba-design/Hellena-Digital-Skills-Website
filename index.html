Reflection

I have chosen to focus on data & digital literacy, problem-solving, and curiosity because together they form a strong foundation for becoming a confident digital specialist. I want my activity to produce clear, usable results, not just learning for its own sake, so my goal is to turn new skills into simple outputs (charts, a dashboard, short reports, or small demos) that others can use to make decisions or to improve a process. That is how I will create impact and deliver the objectives of my Award project: by producing evidence-backed, repeatable work that shows real progress.

What the skills mean to me

  • Data & digital literacy: the ability to find, clean, analyze, and present information so people can understand it. This includes spreadsheets, simple code, dashboards, and clear visuals.
  • Problem solving: a step-by-step way to move from spotting an issue to testing and applying a practical solution, defining the problem, gathering facts, trying options, and learning from the results.
  • Curiosity: an ongoing habit of asking good questions, trying small experiments, and turning interest into finished small projects instead of just browsing.

The plan

  • Complete weekly mini projects for steady practice.
  • Follow a structured learning path for intermediate Python or R, focused on data cleaning and analysis.
  • Take short courses on reproducible workflows (notebooks, basic Git) via LinkedIn Learning or Coursera.
  • Apply a defined test reflection routine to at least two real problems during the year, documenting steps and results.
  • Keep a weekly learning journal with one question and one takeaway, and attend a relevant seminar monthly, turning one idea from each seminar into a tiny project.
  • Request monthly feedback from a mentor to stay accountable.
